Class Information

Computer B

Mr. Snowberger
Period 1

This course offers a deep dive into the essentials of website development. We’ll begin with a look HTML, and CSS, and JavaScript - the de facto foundational web programming languages.

Topics

  1. HTML
    • Text tags
    • Links & Lists
    • Images & Media
    • Tables
    • Forms
  2. CSS
    • Rational CSS Structure
      1. Normalization / reset
      2. Global rules (vars, elements, defaults, etc)
      3. General rules (layouts, areas, reusable components, component parts)
      4. Component rules (extends general rules, styles individual components)
      5. Custom rules (unusual things, one-offs)
      6. Other things (browser hacks, temp code, etc)
    • Rational Property Order
      1. Positioning (position, top, left)
      2. Box model (display, width, margin, flex)
      3. Typography (font-family, weight, size, color, columns)
      4. Visual (list, border, background, transforms)
      5. Animation (transition, animation)
      6. Other (clip, pointer events, SVG features)

ESL Science

Mr. Snowberger
Period 2

The purpose of this class is to help students improve their English language abilities through the use of English in the context of a Science classroom. As such, we will progress through our textbook, The ESL Science Book, that incorporates reading, writing, critical thinking, and various activities to help students improve their English skills. The class will also incorporate video lessons, report writing, and some quizzes and tests.

Pre-Algebra

Mr. Snowberger
Period 5

This is the first half of a 1-year course in pre-algebra. This math course will equip students with the mathematical skills and problem-solving abilities needed to succeed in algebra and in future mathematical studies.
